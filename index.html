Portfolio Construction & Factor Investing — FPIA Final Report

Portfolio Management · Factor Investing · 2024–2025
Cover page · Click image to open full PDF
Overview

This report was produced as the final deliverable for the FPIA (Financial Portfolio & Investment Analysis) programme. It presents a systematic, research-driven approach to multi-asset portfolio construction, combining quantitative factor strategies with modern risk management frameworks.

The work covers the full investment process — from universe screening and factor signal generation, through portfolio optimisation and risk budgeting, to performance attribution and stress testing — grounded in both academic literature and practical implementation considerations.

Key Topics

The report examines equity factor premia (value, momentum, quality, low-volatility), risk-parity and mean–variance optimisation, drawdown control, and multi-asset diversification across equities, fixed income, and alternatives. Transaction-cost-aware backtesting and walk-forward validation are used throughout to ensure robustness.

50ETF Options Strategy Backtesting & Construction

Quantitative Finance · Options · Delta Hedging · 2016–2024
Strategy overview — cumulative hedged P&L (2016–2024)
Overview

This project constructs and validates a systematic options trading strategy using the CSI 50ETF as the underlying asset. The core thesis: go long on Call ITM and Put OTM options (positive expected P&L from hedging), while shorting Call OTM and Put ITM options — all delta-hedged via a synthetic futures position.

The strategy is first validated through a full market backtest covering all contracts from 2016 to 2024, then refined into a realistic portfolio simulation that selects only the nearest at-the-money contracts across near, mid, and far expiry buckets.

Methodology

Daily P&L is computed as PnL = Vt − Vt−1 − (St − St−1) × Δt−1, where the synthetic futures price is derived from put-call parity (S = K + P − C). Contracts are classified by moneyness (ITM/OTM) and expiry type (near / mid / far term), with cumulative P&L tracked across all six groups. The portfolio simulation replicates real holding conditions: 3 contracts per type per expiry bucket, rolled at 14:55 daily close.

Stress-period analysis (e.g. the Feb 2019 spike) decomposes intraday Gamma, Vega, and Delta exposures to explain tail behaviour and validate the hedge's robustness under extreme market moves.

Key Findings

Far-term contracts consistently outperform near-term across both long and short legs. The live portfolio simulation shows smoother P&L than the raw backtest, confirming that ATM synthetic hedging is more precise than aggregated all-contract hedging. The strategy demonstrates positive risk-adjusted returns over the full 8-year sample.
